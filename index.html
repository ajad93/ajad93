          How to revise · Phase 1 · Maths
          01Read the textbook worked example — don't just skim it, follow every line and ask yourself why each step was done.
          02Close the book and redo the example from scratch. If you get stuck, look at one line only, then continue. This is the single most effective thing you can do.
          03Do 3–5 exercise questions per sub-topic — enough to check you can do it, not so many you're grinding.
          04Write down anything you got wrong and the correct method. Don't move on until you understand why.
          05No formula sheets during content phase — if you need to look something up, that's a signal to memorise it now.

          How to revise · Phase 1 · Physics
          01Notes first, but keep them minimal — one page per topic max. Key equations, definitions, diagrams. Don't write out the textbook.
          02For every equation, do a worked calculation immediately. Physics content revision that doesn't involve numbers is almost useless.
          03Definitions must be exact. Write the definition, cover it, write it again. Edexcel mark schemes require specific wording — "proportional to" vs "equal to" will cost you marks.
          04Draw diagrams by hand — field lines, circuit diagrams, wave diagrams. Looking at them is not the same as drawing them.
          05After each topic, do 2–3 past paper questions on that topic only to check you actually understood it, not just read it.

          How to revise · Phase 2 · Economics
          01Write timed KAA paragraphs — set 12 minutes for an 8-mark answer, 20 minutes for a 12-mark. Don't plan for too long; the planning should be automatic by now.
          02Evaluation is where the marks are — "it depends on the PED", "in the short run vs long run", "this conflicts with the objective of equality". Practice adding a genuine evaluative point to every answer, not just restating the point.
          03For data response — always extract a figure and put it in context. Don't just quote it: "Output fell by 2.3%, suggesting a significant contraction in AD."
          04Compare your answers to mark scheme indicative content — but remember the mark scheme shows examples, not the only route to full marks. Focus on whether your reasoning chain is complete.

          Phase 3 Past papers 25 May – 14 Jun
          How to revise · Phase 3 · Economics
          01Plan before you write — spend 3–4 minutes outlining your KAA structure for each essay question. Unplanned essays meander and lose evaluation marks.
          02Section A data response — read the extract carefully before the questions. Underline relevant data. The extract is there to be used; answers that ignore it get capped.
          03After marking, look at your evaluations specifically — did you just list counterpoints, or did you actually explain why the original argument might be limited? That distinction is the difference between a C and a B.
          04Build a bank of 5–6 strong evaluation points per theme that you can adapt to almost any question — e.g. "depends on the price elasticity", "government failure may offset the policy benefit", "short-run vs long-run trade-offs".
